Case III - H. & W., Higuruma v. J. & L., Sukuna

May 25, 1988: H and W purchased a house and lot on installment from spouses J and L. The property was covered by Transfer Certificate of Title (TCT) No. 3250 in the name of J.

July 1988: A Deed of Absolute Sale was executed. J and L bound themselves to deliver the title, free from liens and encumbrances, within 15 days from full payment. They informed H and W that the lot was still under a mother title and promised to give the separate title after construction was completed.

August 1988: The keys to the property were delivered to H and W. They moved in, applied for a telephone connection, and insured the house.

June 15, 1989: Despite having already sold the property to H and W, J and L mortgaged the property to F Corp. for P250,000.

October 23, 1990: H and W filed a case for Quieting of Title and Specific Performance against J and L.

October 7, 1992: F Corp. foreclosed on the property, allegedly in defiance of a Writ of Preliminary Injunction issued by the lower court. F Corp. was the highest bidder at the public auction.

After the auction: Due to J and L's failure to redeem the property, F Corp. executed an Affidavit of Consolidation and secured Certificate of Title No. 69049 in its name.
