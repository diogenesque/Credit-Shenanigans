Case II - R., Igunako v. E., Mueran

September 13, 1984, E claims purchase of the property from spouses H and W. Due to employment abroad, the Deed of Absolute Sale was placed in the name of the brother, A.

April 8, 1985, A executed a Deed of Donation over the property in favor of E’s minor son, J. J reportedly received the fruits of the property from this time onward.

September 14, 1988, A mortgaged the property to R.

September 30, 1988, The mortgage between A and R was amended.

June 1992, E learned that the property had been extrajudicially foreclosed and filed a complaint to annul the mortgage, arguing A had no right to mortgage the property.

R filed an answer claiming status as a mortgagee in good faith, having relied on the fact that the land title and deed of sale were in A's name.
